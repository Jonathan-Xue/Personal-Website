About Me

Hi! My name is Jonathan Xue and I am an incoming college freshmen with a passion for computer science. You're probably here to learn a bit more about me, so read on!


I'm a member of the 2017-18 Illinois Junior Academy Of Science Student Board, which hosts the annual Illinois State Science Fair. We aim to expose students of all ages to the STEM field, as individuals explore personal interests through research and experimentation.


Hackathons have been one of the greatest influences on my life thus far. There is something magical about being in an environment which inspires success; a place where I can focus on developing a product through teamwork and competition. Some of my fondest moments have come from these caffeine-full events, whether it be trying to find a team at Huskie Hacks or debugging our implementation of Microsoft Cognitive Services at MHacks.


Outside of tech, I also enjoy playingg the piano in my spare time. I started when I was eight, and ever since then, have fallen in love with the crisp, clear tones, each hanging with various intensities. Music is a form of expression; I can always rely on Bach's Preludes & Fugues, Chopin's Nocturnes, among countless others to help me through my joys and sorrows.

Notable Projects

Watchdog

Built at MHacks X, Watchdog is a platform created to increase engagement in classrooms. Using a camera and Microsoft's Cognitive Services, Watchdog monitors a classroom and offers the teacher live, in-depth analytics on the current state of the room, by collecting continuous data on the emotions and engagement of the students.

Learn more
Aspecto

Built at Huskie Hacks - Health & Wellness, Aspecto is a platform aimed towards improving employees' mental and physical health within the workplace. Aspecto analyzes inputs from the user's microphone/camera to provide workers with a comprehensive overview of their emotional state, while generating alerts regarding healthy habits such as posture and eye exercises.

Learn more
Exterminat0r

Built at Hack Ridge, Exterminat0r is an experimental first-person shooter virtual reality game. It consists of two separate modes: aracde and survival. In arcade mode, the user has 60 seconds to shoot as many static blocks as possible. In survival mode, the user is placed in a custom map against infinite waves of enemy spawns. Motions are controlled via an X-Box Controller.

Learn more
Caveat

Built at CodeDay Chicago and Chicago Hacks, Caveat is a platform which serves comprehensive crime analytics within the city of Chicago. Using information from the Chicago Data Portal, a heat map is generated to facilitate an intuitive understanding regarding areas of high risk. Further analysis is also offered, detailing crime types/frequency in the immediate vincinity.

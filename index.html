About Me

Hi! My name is Jonathan Xue and I am a college freshmen with a passion for computer science. You're probably here to learn a bit more about me, so read on!


I'm a member of the 2017-18 Illinois Junior Academy Of Science Student Board, which hosts the annual Illinois State Science Fair. We aim to expose students of all ages to the STEM field, as individuals explore personal interests through research and experimentation.


Hackathons have been one of the greatest influences on my life thus far. There is something magical about being in an environment which inspires success; a place where I can focus on developing a product through teamwork and competition. Some of my fondest moments have come from these caffeine-full events, whether it be trying to find a team at Huskie Hacks or debugging our implementation of Microsoft Cognitive Services at MHacks.


Outside of tech, I also enjoy playing the piano in my spare time. I started when I was eight, and ever since then, have fallen in love with the crisp, clear tones, each hanging with various intensities. Music is a form of expression; I can always rely on Bach's Preludes & Fugues, Chopin's Nocturnes, among countless others to help me through my joys and sorrows.

Notable Projects

Stud-Vision

Built at PennApps XVIII, Stud-Vision is a mobile application aimed towards increasing student comprehension via augmented reality. Stud-Vision analyzes live and static inputs through the camera, and associates key diagrams and text with the corresponding 3D models. Users are able to interact in real-time, zooming in/out and rotating models to facilitate a better understanding of core concepts..

Watchdog

Built at MHacks X, Watchdog is a platform created to increase engagement in classrooms. Using a camera and Microsoft's Cognitive Services, Watchdog monitors a classroom and offers the teacher live, in-depth analytics on the current state of the room through collecting continuous data on the emotions and engagement of the students. The data is aggregated to display trends over time.

Aspecto

Built at Huskie Hacks - Health & Wellness, Aspecto is a platform aimed towards improving employees' mental and physical health within the workplace. Aspecto analyzes inputs from the user's microphone and camera to provide workers with a comprehensive overview of their physical and emotional state. It uses the data to generate live alerts regarding healthy habits such as posture and eye exercises.

Caveat

Built at CodeDay Chicago and Chicago Hacks, Caveat is a platform which serves comprehensive crime analytics within the city of Chicago. Using information from the Chicago Data Portal, a heat map is generated to facilitate an intuitive understanding regarding areas of high risk. Further analysis is also offered, detailing crime types and frequencies in the immediate vincinity.
